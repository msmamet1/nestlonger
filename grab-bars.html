Why it matters

A fall changes everything. A grab bar changes the odds.

One in four older adults falls each year, and most falls happen in the bathroom. The fix is unglamorous, cheap, and proven. The hard part is getting it done before something happens.

The fix is boring. The math is not.

One properly anchored bar in the right spot reduces falls in that location by up to 80%. Most bathrooms need two, one inside the shower, one by the toilet.

The work takes two hours. The hard part is finding someone who anchors into the framing instead of the drywall, and who shows up on time. That's our job.

Insurance coverage

A lot of families pay nothing.

Most people don't realize their parent's plan already covers this. Here are the four places it usually shows up, we'll check them with you.

Often covered

Medicare Advantage

Many plans now cover home modifications under their supplemental benefits. We help you check yours.

Often covered

VA Benefits

If your parent is a veteran, the HISA grant can fully cover bathroom modifications including grab bars.

Sometimes covered

Long-term care insurance

Most LTC policies reimburse home safety improvements with a doctor's note. We help you submit.

Often covered

UnitedHealthcare

UHC's home safety benefit reimburses qualifying grab bar installs through partnered providers.

Coverage varies by plan and state. We won't promise reimbursement, we'll tell you what's likely and help you ask the right question.
