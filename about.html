About NestLonger

Families deserve a trusted guide before the crisis — not after.

Most people figure out aging-in-place by accident — after a fall, after a hospital stay, after a phone call from a neighbor. NestLonger exists so adult children can get ahead of it.

Why we built this

The home safety industry is a maze. Aging-in-place modifications are scattered across handymen, contractors, occupational therapists, insurance plans, state programs, and a long tail of well-meaning nonprofits. The information is everywhere and trustworthy almost nowhere.

Meanwhile, the people who need this most — adult children, age 45–60, helping a parent — are the most time-poor demographic in America. They have full-time jobs, kids of their own, and a parent who insists they're "fine." When they finally start looking, they're already behind.

We started with the simplest, highest-impact intervention: grab bars. We're growing into ADUs next, then home modifications more broadly. The model is the same one that worked for solar (EnergySage) and auto insurance (EverQuote): own intent through honest content, refer families to vetted local pros, get paid only when something good happens.

How we work — four rules.

  • 01

    Address the adult child, not the parent

    The buyer is the daughter, the son, the niece. We write to them — and we never call their parent a "senior" or "loved one."

  • 02

    Lead with the outcome, not the fear

    A grab bar reduces falls by 80%. That's the headline. The category is heavy enough; we don't need to add to it.

  • 03

    Refer to people we trust

    Every partner is licensed, insured, and aging-in-place trained. We'd send our own parent. That's the bar.

  • 04

    Get paid on outcomes

    We're paid by partners on closed referrals — not on leads, not on subscriptions. Our incentive is your incentive.
