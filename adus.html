Is an ADU right for you

More family time, less institutional care.

ADUs aren't right for every family or every lot. They're the right move when proximity matters more than the perfect home, and when you'd rather invest in a permanent solution than a recurring monthly bill.

A second front door, not a wing on yours.

The right ADU gives your parent privacy, autonomy, and dignity — their own kitchen, their own routines, their own friends visiting at the door. You're across the yard, not across the country.

Done well, it's also the most flexible asset on the property: caregiver suite today, rental income later, adult-child landing pad after that.

Cost & financing

Four ways families actually pay for it.

Most ADUs land between $150K and $300K all-in. Here's how families fund them — we'll help you stack them.

1

Cash-out refinance

If you have meaningful equity, this is usually the cheapest capital. We help you compare to a HELOC.

2

HELOC

Flexible draw, interest-only during construction. Typical for projects under $200K.

3

Renovation loan

Fannie Mae HomeStyle and FHA 203(k) cover ADU construction in many states.

4

State ADU programs

California, Oregon, and a growing list now offer ADU-specific loans and grants. We track them.

We're not a lender and we don't take a cut of your financing. We connect you with vetted brokers who specialize in ADU projects.
